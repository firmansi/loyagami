Nationally, more than 1.35 million households that earned at least $ 150,000 per year became renters between 2007 and 2017. While they can afford a home, most economically well-off Americans prefer to rent a house.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

The number of US households earning at least $ 150,000 per year who choose to rent rather than buy a home increased 175 percent between 2007 and 2017, according to an analysis of apartment search site RentCafe, which uses data from the Census Bureau.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

This type of wealthy tenant contradicts the long-held assumption that Americans are renting housing primarily because they can’t afford a home.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
